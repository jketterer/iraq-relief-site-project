Overview

Beginnings

The war began in January 2014 when the militant group ISIS infiltrated and captured the cities of Fallujah and Ramadi after months of escalating violence.

With the ultimate goal of capturing Iraq's capital city, Baghdad, ISIS continued to capture territory. Throughout the rest of 2014, Kurdish and US forces assist the Iraqi government in repelling ISIS attacks on various cities.

ISIS managed to capture the cities of Mosul and Tikrit before the Iraqi government was able to launch a full assault against the guerilla troops.

The Fighting Continues

Throughout 2015 and 2016, the Iraqi government and ISIS fought for control of Tikrit and the Anbar province. The Iraqi government managed to retake Tikrit after a month of fighting, but ISIS captured the province of Anbar less than a month later.

In 2016, the Iraqi government was able to retake Fallujah and Mosul, marking huge defeats for ISIS. The offensive continued in 2017 as the Iraqi Army launched sweep operations to retake what ISIS territory remained in the country. On November 17th, 2017, Iraq captured ISIS's last two strongholds, and officially ended the war on December 9th.
